Help Stray Animals


Intro

I have always loved small animals, but because some of my family members have rhinitis, it is not suitable to keep pets. So I will go out and feed the kittens and puppies around the house, and I really enjoy spending time with them. But during this process, I also saw stray animals fighting for food, seeing infected wounds on their bodies, and even seeing some people taking pleasure in harming them. This makes me feel sad and angry, hoping to do something to help them.

After further investigation, I found that the number of stray animals exceeded my imagination. According to data from 2019, there are over 600 million stray animals worldwide, with cats and dogs being the majority. And you may not imagine that among dogs, the number of stray dogs accounts for 33% of the total number of dogs.



WHY?

Animal Welfare

Stray animals often live in extremely difficult and dangerous conditions, without adequate food, water and shelter. By rescuing them, their quality of life can be improved and their pain and suffering can be alleviated.

Animal Control

Controlling the number of stray animals can help prevent over breeding and overcrowding, reducing stray animals in cities.

Disease Control

Stray animals can be vectors for spreading diseases, such as rabies. By capturing, treating and vaccinating stray animals, the spread of these diseases can be reduced and the health of communities protected.

Community Safety

Some stray animals may become aggressive or dangerous, posing a threat to community residents. This threat can be reduced by rescuing and providing appropriate shelter.

Moral Obligations

As humans, we have a responsibility to treat other living creatures with kindness and respect. Rescuing stray animals is a moral obligation that demonstrates our care and concern for their well-being.
